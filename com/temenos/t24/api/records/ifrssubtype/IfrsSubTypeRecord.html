      • getTerm

        public TField getTerm()
        Returns the Term

        Field description: Option for determining calculation of the EIR and amortised cost (i.e. do we calculate up to the next rate fix date if we know it or the maturity date) When the field TERM is set as 'None' or 'Full', the system will calculate the EIR for the entire term of the contract. This will be applicable mainly in case of the fixed rate contracts, where the contract rate remains unchanged throughout the life of the contract. When the field TERM is set as 'Short', the system will calculate the EIR up to the next rate fixing date and income will be amortised over this period. When the next rate is fixed a new EIR will be calculated for the next amortisation period. When the field TERM is set as EXPECTED it means this is applicable for contracts with an expected term. The Expected term details will be passed to the Cashflow engine. Cashflow engine will consolidate the contractual cashflows to fit into the expected term for a set of cashflows required for the EIR calculation. All contractual cashflows beyond the expected life end (based on the Expected term and the Start date) will be consolidated under the expected life end. The system will calculate the EIR up to the expected life end date, and the Amortised cost will be calculated as the NPV of the cashflows till the Expected life end. Validations:

        Valid Inputs are SHORT, FULL and EXPECTED.

        Returns:
