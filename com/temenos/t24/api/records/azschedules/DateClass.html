      • getScheduleBal

        public TField getScheduleBal()
        Returns the ScheduleBal

        Field description: The field indicates BALANCE in the account as per SCHEDULES. The balance indicates a FUTURE position assuming a pattern of INFLOW over the period. For Ex:=>A Savings plan with a monthly installment of Rs.1000 is opened on 01.02.02. The schedules ASSUME that every month the installments are received and projects a balance of 2000 as on 01.03.02 3000 as on 01.04.02 4000 as on 01.05.02 and so on and the interest amounts are calculated and capitalized accordingly. But in reality the inflows might not have come for the month of March and April which makes the actual balance as 1000 (while the scheduled balance on that date would be 3000) In case of LOAN accounts the SCHEDULED balance will be diminishing over the periods assuming a fixed pattern of repayment over the periods. Validation Rules Rule 1:- It is simply a projection of FUTURE cash inflow into the account Rule 2:- Actual balance may differ from the scheduled balance.

      • getTypeP

        public TField getTypeP()
        Returns the TypeP

        Field description: This field indicates the type of schedule defined by the user in AZ account, for repayment of Principal amount. In the case of AZ loan account, the amount shown here is the amount of Principal Repayment to be made by the customer on the specified date. According to the type of repayment like Annuity, Linear and Others, the schedule takes care of defining the Principal repayment. When 'Other' type of Repayment is specified in AZ account, unstructured Principal repayments can be defined in P type schedule by specifying the amount and the date. Based on the schedule types defined in the AZ account, AZ.SCHEDULE is generated by the system. By giving the ID of the AZ account in AZ.SCHEDULE, the complete schedule details of the account can be viewed. Schedule is automatically generated by the system and no user input is allowed . Further, no automatic actions like in LD/ MG are done by the system based on AZ.SCHEDULE . In All in One Module, the SCHEDULE is used only as a reference point, for updating the limit, ADI and comparing the AZ account balance on scheduled dates, for arriving the arrears or the redraw value. The other types of schedules are: A - For defining annuity type of repayment, where a constant repayment amount is generated based on the frequency. It is possible to define multiple 'A' type of schedules within an AZ loan account for different periods during the term of the loan. In such cases, it is necessary to input at least one 'A' type schedule that includes the maturity date and brings the loan balance to zero (or the residual amount). B - This type is used for effecting loan drawdowns on pre-defined dates. On the specified dates the system will transfer the draw down amount to the credit of the nominated account. I - Defines schedule for interest payments. For I type schedule, amount cannot be specified by the user. Total period of all I type schedules are validated against the Maximum Interest Only period defined for the AZ account. R- This type defines the Interest rate revision on a defined date. This type takes care of both variable interest and fixed interest based on the percentage of outstanding amount as well as on various periods of the loan. In order to change the interest rates defaulted from the AZ Product Parameter, a R type schedule can be defined at the start of loan itself, with new values.         Field description: This field indicates the type of schedule defined by the user in AZ account, for repayment of Principal amount. In the case of AZ loan account, the amount shown here is the amount of Principal Repayment to be made by the customer on the specified date. According to the type of repayment like Annuity, Linear and Others, the schedule takes care of defining the Principal repayment. When 'Other' type of Repayment is specified in AZ account, unstructured Principal repayments can be defined in P type schedule by specifying the amount and the date. Based on the schedule types defined in the AZ account, AZ.SCHEDULE is generated by the system. By giving the ID of the AZ account in AZ.SCHEDULE, the complete schedule details of the account can be viewed. Schedule is automatically generated by the system and no user input is allowed . Further, no automatic actions like in LD/ MG are done by the system based on AZ.SCHEDULE . In All in One Module, the SCHEDULE is used only as a reference point, for updating the limit, ADI and comparing the AZ account balance on scheduled dates, for arriving the arrears or the redraw value. The other types of schedules are: A - For defining annuity type of repayment, where a constant repayment amount is generated based on the frequency. It is possible to define multiple 'A' type of schedules within an AZ loan account for different periods during the term of the loan. In such cases, it is necessary to input at least one 'A' type schedule that includes the maturity date and brings the loan balance to zero (or the residual amount). B - This type is used for effecting loan drawdowns on pre-defined dates. On the specified dates the system will transfer the draw down amount to the credit of the nominated account. I - Defines schedule for interest payments. For I type schedule, amount cannot be specified by the user. Total period of all I type schedules are validated against the Maximum Interest Only period defined for the AZ account. R- This type defines the Interest rate revision on a defined date. This type takes care of both variable interest and fixed interest based on the percentage of outstanding amount as well as on various periods of the loan. In order to change the interest rates defaulted from the AZ Product Parameter, a R type schedule can be defined at the start of loan itself, with new values.
