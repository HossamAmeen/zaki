        public TField getBalNoOffset()

        Field description: Specifies the balance required for calculating notional credit interest for offsetting against charges, for Accounts in the Currency specified in the corresponding OFFSET.CURRENCY. Default values (in local currency equivalent) defined in DEFAULT.MIN.AV.BAL, DEFAULT.DAY.BASIS, DEF.BAL.NO.OFFSET and DEF.LOW.AMT.CHARGE are used for Accounts in Currencies with no input in OFFSET.CURRENCY, MIN.AV.BAL, DAY.BASIS, BAL.NO.CREDIT and LOW.AMT.CHARGE. The Average or Minimum Balance for the application period is determined (depending on the OFFSET.BAL.TYPE). If the result is less than the valuespecified in this field, no offsetting credit interest is calculated. __________________________________________________ (1) 0-14 numeric characters plus a decimal point.

      • getDayBasis

        public TField getDayBasis()
        Returns the DayBasis

        Field description: Specifies the Day Basis for calculating notional credit interest for offsetting charges, for Accounts in the Currency specified in the corresponding OFFSET.CURRENCY. The multivalue group of associated Fields OFFSET.CURRENCY, MIN.AV.BAL, DAY.BASIS, BAL.NO.CREDIT and LOW.AMT.CHARGE is used to specify details for waiving charges and calculating offsets for Accounts in specific Currencies. Default values (in local currency equivalent) defined in DEFAULT.MIN.AV.BAL, DEFAULT.DAY.BASIS, DEF.BAL.NO.OFFSET and DEF.LOW.AMT.CHARGE are used for Accounts in Currencies with no input in OFFSET.CURRENCY, MIN.AV.BAL, DAY.BASIS, BAL.NO.CREDIT and LOW.AMT.CHARGE. If PERCT.FOR.OFFSET is defined, the Average or Minimum Balance for the application period is determined (depending on OFFSET.BAL.TYPE) and the notional credit interest is calculated using the PERCT.FOR.OFFSET as the rate and the Day Basis defined in this field. If A (360/360) or F (360/360) is specified each month will be treated as having 30 days. In months with 31 days, the 30th is ignored, and in February the last day is counted as 3 days, or 2 in a leap year. __________________________________________________ (1) 'A 360/360', 'B 366/360', 'E 366/365' or 'F 360/365' or 'A', 'B', 'E' or 'F'.
