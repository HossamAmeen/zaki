        Field description: Specifies the next date and subsequent frequency for producing the regular (cycle 1) account statement. The field could be multi-valued in which case, a statement containing entries from the statement last printed in the cycle 1, would be generated for each multi-valued date. This field is automatically generated by the system when a new Account is opened to ensure that statements are produced for every Account. It can be altered manually. This field cannot be input if the account has a passbook. The first part of the field specifies the next date on which the Cycle 1 statement will be produced, the 2nd part specifies the frequency for producing Cycle 1 statements. The next date is updated automatically by the system every time a Cycle 1 statement is produced. If the next date falls on a non-working day, the statement is produced on the previous working day. The date is then updated as if the statement had been produced on the day specified, i.e. the cycle is not affected. When amending this field manually, if the next date is omitted, the system automatically generates it as follows: FREQUENCY NEXT DATE B(snss) (Every business day) Next working day after today T(wmth) (Twice a month, 15th and last day) The next 15th or month end from today, whichever is sooner Wn (Every n weeks) n weeks from today It is possible to enter the above frequency options by the letter alone. The date will then default in accordance with the frequency rules as detailed above, e.g. option 1 would be the next working day from the system date. It is also possible to input a frequency with a date. Mnndd (Every nn months on the dd day) If today's date is less than dd, the next date is day dd in nn months from last month, otherwise it is day dd in nn months form the current month. If this date does not exist (e.g. 30th February) the previous day is assumed (28th or 29th February). Note - Checks are performed to ensure that the first cycle statements are requested for production not more than 12 months from the last requested date or if the account is being opened, the account opening date. Should either of these situations be encountered, it gives ride to an override situation but you may continue subject to being able to satisfy the relevant override class. However, the frequency cannot be more than 12 months. Validation Rules: This field is in two parts. Next Statement Date: 1-9 Date characters. Default value calculated by the system depending on the Frequency. Frequency :1-5 type SS (uppercase alpha or numeric, first character alpha) characters. The frequency cannot be longer than 12 months. The next date must not be less than today's date. The next date may be more than 12 months from the date of the last statement, or the date of opening the Account if no statements have been produced yet but is subject to override. This field is mandatory apart from when an account is indicated as a PASSBOOK account When multi-valued, a frequency of "BSNSS" could not be duplicated in two multi-values of cycle 1, irrespective of next date. The first part of the field specifies the next date on which the Cycle 1 statement will be produced, the 2nd part specifies the frequency for producing Cycle 1 statements. The next date is updated automatically by the system every time a Cycle 1 statement is produced. If the next date falls on a non-working day, the statement is produced on the previous working day. The date is then updated as if the statement had been produced on the day specified, i.e. the cycle is not affected. When amending this field manually, if the next date is omitted, the system automatically generates it as follows: FREQUENCY NEXT DATE B(snss) (Every business day) Next working day after today T(wmth) (Twice a month, 15th and last day) The next 15th or month end from today, whichever is sooner Wn (Every n weeks) n weeks from today It is possible to enter the above frequency options by the letter alone. The date will then default in accordance with the frequency rules as detailed above, e.g. option 1 would be the next working day from the system date. It is also possible to input a frequency with a date. Mnndd (Every nn months on the dd day) If today's date is less than dd, the next date is day dd in nn months from last month, otherwise it is day dd in nn months form the current month. If this date does not exist (e.g. 30th February) the previous day is assumed (28th or 29th February). Note - Checks are performed to ensure that the first cycle statements are requested for production not more than 12 months from the last requested date or if the account is being opened, the account opening date. However, the frequency cannot be more than 12 months. Validation Rules: This field is in two parts. Next Statement Date: 1-9 Date characters.       • setStmtFqu1

        public void setStmtFqu1(java.lang.CharSequence param, int idx)
        Set the specified StmtFqu1 to a specific position in collection.
        Throws an UnsupportedOperationException if the object is not modifiable.

        Field description: Specifies the next date and subsequent frequency for producing the regular (cycle 1) account statement. The field could be multi-valued in which case, a statement containing entries from the statement last printed in the cycle 1, would be generated for each multi-valued date. This field is automatically generated by the system when a new Account is opened to ensure that statements are produced for every Account. It can be altered manually. This field cannot be input if the account has a passbook. The first part of the field specifies the next date on which the Cycle 1 statement will be produced, the 2nd part specifies the frequency for producing Cycle 1 statements. The next date is updated automatically by the system every time a Cycle 1 statement is produced. If the next date falls on a non-working day, the statement is produced on the previous working day. The date is then updated as if the statement had been produced on the day specified, i.e. the cycle is not affected. When amending this field manually, if the next date is omitted, the system automatically generates it as follows: FREQUENCY NEXT DATE B(snss) (Every business day) Next working day after today T(wmth) (Twice a month, 15th and last day) The next 15th or month end from today, whichever is sooner Wn (Every n weeks) n weeks from today It is possible to enter the above frequency options by the letter alone. The date will then default in accordance with the frequency rules as detailed above, e.g. option 1 would be the next working day from the system date. It is also possible to input a frequency with a date. Mnndd (Every nn months on the dd day) If today's date is less than dd, the next date is day dd in nn months from last month, otherwise it is day dd in nn months form the current month. If this date does not exist (e.g. 30th February) the previous day is assumed (28th or 29th February). Note - Checks are performed to ensure that the first cycle statements are requested for production not more than 12 months from the last requested date or if the account is being opened, the account opening date. Should either of these situations be encountered, it gives ride to an override situation but you may continue subject to being able to satisfy the relevant override class. However, the frequency cannot be more than 12 months. Validation Rules: This field is in two parts. Next Statement Date: 1-9 Date characters. Default value calculated by the system depending on the Frequency. Frequency :1-5 type SS (uppercase alpha or numeric, first character alpha) characters. The frequency cannot be longer than 12 months. The next date must not be less than today's date. The next date may be more than 12 months from the date of the last statement, or the date of opening the Account if no statements have been produced yet but is subject to override. This field is mandatory apart from when an account is indicated as a PASSBOOK account When multi-valued, a frequency of "BSNSS" could not be duplicated in two multi-values of cycle 1, irrespective of next date. When multi-valued, a frequency of "Mnndd" could not be duplicated in two multi-values of cycle 1, irrespective of next date.
